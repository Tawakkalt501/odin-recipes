Turkey and Yam Peppersoup

Description

Turkey and Yam Peppersoup is a hearty Nigerian comfort dish that combines rich, spicy broth with tender turkey cuts and soft yam chunks. The bold aroma of local peppersoup spice, black pepper, scent leaf, basil leaf and grated ginger makes this peppersoup a flavorful and soothing meal, especially on cold days or when you need something nourishing. The turkey adds a meaty depth, while the yam absorbs all that peppery, spicy goodness to create a satisfying bite every time.

This dish is perfect for when you're craving something warm, spicy, and filling without too much fuss. Whether enjoyed as a light dinner or served at gatherings, turkey and yam peppersoup is the kind of meal that hits the soul and wakes up the tastebuds. Best served hot, it pairs beautifully with a chilled drink and a cozy vibe.

Ingredients

  • 8- 10 Turkey Pieces
  • Chopped Yam
  • Pepper Soup Spice (I used 3 tbsp)
  • Crayfish powder (optional)
  • 2 tbsp black Pepper
  • Scotch Bonnet Peppers
  • 1 Onion
  • Scent leaves/Basil leaf
  • Grated Ginger
  • 2 Knorr chicken seasoning
  • Water 1.5 litres
  • Salt to taste

Steps

  1. Wash the turkey pieces thoroughly, then transfer into a pot, add salt, knorr chicken, grated ginger, chopped onions.
  2. Mix thoroughly, then steam on low heat for 5 minutes
  3. Now, add the chopped yam, enough water to cover, then cook for 15 minutes. They yam i used here was old yam and very though, hence the decision to cook along with the turkey from the start.
  4. After 15 minutes, add the scotch bonnet, ground pepper, crayfish, black pepper. Taste for seasoning, adjust if necessary. Mix, then cook till both yam and turkey are soft
  5. Then add the scent leaves/basil leaves, simmer for 2 minutes..
